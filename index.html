Constitutional AI

Constitutional AI (CAI) is a technique developed by Anthropic for training AI assistants. Rather than relying solely on human feedback, CAI uses a set of principles (a "constitution") to guide the AI's behavior during training. The process involves two main phases: a supervised learning phase where the model critiques and revises its own responses based on constitutional principles, and a reinforcement learning phase where the model is trained using AI-generated feedback rather than human labels. This approach allows for more scalable alignment while reducing the need for extensive human annotation. The constitution includes principles from sources like the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, as well as Anthropic-specific guidelines for helpfulness and harmlessness. CAI has been shown to produce models that are both more helpful and less harmful than those trained with standard RLHF approaches.

Responsible Scaling

Anthropic's Responsible Scaling Policy defines AI Safety Levels (ASL) that guide how the company develops and deploys increasingly capable AI systems.
